
## Something meaningful that you have done in line with your values
In one paragraph, provide an example of something meaningful that you have done in line with your values. Examples could include past work, volunteering, civic engagement, community organizing, donations, family support, etc.*

Two commitments reflect what I value most: using my skills where they matter and quietly showing up for the people around me. On the technical side, I have been developing Bommie Toolkit in my own time, a free, open-source tool for automated 3D reconstruction of coral reefs, built so scientists can use it straight out of the box without needing deep technical expertise. I built this in collaboration with TRACC (Tropical Research and Conservation Centre), a volunteer-funded NGO restoring fish-bombed reefs in Borneo. On a more personal level, I have deliberately structured my finances to set aside 5% of my salary as a standing fund to support family and friends in need, no hesitation, no keeping score.

## Why Anthropic?
My career has followed a consistent arc: from developing core algorithms during my PhD, improving VSLAM accuracy, robustness and efficiency, to building widely-used research infrastructure during my postdoc, to applying these techniques to problems with direct real-world consequence: 3D reconstruction of coral reefs for conservation, drone monitoring for climate research.

Over the last months that arc has extended into AI agents. I've been building multi-agent systems to maintain and extend VSLAM-LAB, teaching agents to add new datasets and baselines on demand, and designing an algorithm gym for AI-driven VSLAM fine-tuning.

What draws me to Anthropic is that interpretability is treated as empirical research problems, systematic, measurable, and honest about what we don't yet understand. My latest work on ground-truth-free evaluation sits in exactly this space: assessing whether a system is working correctly when you cannot directly measure correctness. That class of problem is one I've spent years on in robotics. I'm drawn to working on it at a scale where the stakes are much higher.

The structure also fits. Anthropic works as a single cohesive team on a small number of large-scale efforts, that matches how I do my best work. Some of my strongest results have come from direct technical discussion with colleagues. I'm looking for a place where that's the norm rather than the exception.
